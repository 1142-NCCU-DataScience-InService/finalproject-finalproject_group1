## Presentation Structure

A complete presentation must cover the following four sections:

1. **Goal / Input** — What is your objective and what data do you use?
2. **Modeling** — What methods and models did you apply?
3. **Results** — What performance did you achieve?
4. **Demo** — Live or reproducible demonstration.

---

## Section Details

### 1. Input

- Data source
- Input format
- Preprocessing steps (ref: hw5)
  - Handling missing data
  - Scaling values

### 2. Modeling

- Which method(s) did you use?
- What is the null/baseline model for comparison?
- How did you train your model? (ref: hw3)
  - Cross-validation, or a separate held-out dataset
- Optimal hyperparameters and how they were found

### 3. Results

- Performance evaluation (ref: hw2)
  - Which metric did you choose?
  - Why is that metric appropriate for your data?
  - Is your improvement over the baseline statistically significant?

### 4. Demo

- Online visualization (ref: hw4)
- How to reproduce your results?
- What was the most challenging part of the project?
